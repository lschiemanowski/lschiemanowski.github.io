Lothar Schiemanowski

I'm a postdoc in the geometry group of the University of Kiel and a member of SPP 2026. My work is concerned with Riemannian geometry of Einstein and special holonomy manifolds using the methods of geometric analysis. A more recent interest of mine are moduli spaces of harmonic maps and Higgs bundles and their hyperkähler geometry.

Publications and preprints

  • Topology of asymptotically conical Calabi--Yau and G2 manifolds and desingularization of nearly Kähler and nearly G2 conifolds
        May 2022    arXiv, to appear in J. Geom. Anal.

    This article reports on obstructions to a gluing construction of nearly Kähler and nearly G2 manifolds. It is part of a larger project aimed at finding new inhomogeneous examples of such manifolds.

  • On limit spaces of Riemannian manifolds with volume and integral curvature bounds
        June 2020    arXiv, to appear in Comm. Anal. Geom.

    In this paper I investigated the potential limits of Riemannian manifolds with Riemann tensor uniformly bounded in Lp, allowing local collapse. Originally motivated by geometric flows, I developed an independent interest in the question. Using results of Shioya I obtained a particularly pleasant description of the limit spaces of surfaces.

  • Homogeneous spinor flow (joint with Marco Freibert and Hartmut Weiß)
         November 2018     arXiv, Q. J. Math.

    The spinor flow behaves rather differently than curvature flows like the Ricci flow. To gain a more concrete understanding Marco Freibert, Hartmut Weiß and I investigated the behavior of the spinor flow on homogeneous manifolds. This setting is substantially more manageable than the general setting, because the parabolic partial differential equation reduces to a ordinary differential equation. An interesting observation we made is that a certain nearly Kähler manifold is a (globally) stable critical point of the homogeneous spinor flow.

  • Blowup criteria for geometric flows on surfaces
         March 2018     arXiv

    For any geometric flow a fundamental question is how long the solution persists. For many geometric flows, this can be answered by an invocation of the maximum principle. Unfortunately, this does not seem to be the case for the spinor flow. An alternative is to use the regularity properties of parabolic equations. This has to be performed carefully because the underlying manifold may degenerate and therefore also the associated differential operators and their properties. The above paper gives a fairly general approach to this question for geometric flows on closed surfaces. For an alternative presentation you may also consult my thesis.

  • Stability of the spinor flow
         June 2017     arXiv

    The spinor flow was introduced by B. Ammann, H. Weiß and F. Witt to find Ricci flat special holonomy metrics using the spinorial characterization of such metrics. This paper verifies a basic expecation for this flow: given a slight perturbation of such a metric, the flow returns to a (potentially different) Ricci flat special holonomy metric at an exponential rate. This property is known as stability and one can also consider the stability of volume constrained critical points of the spinor flow. Sasaki--Einstein, nearly Kähler and nearly G2 metrics are examples of such critical points. In this case, stability is not generally expected and the paper gives a necessary condition. My thesis also contains these results.
